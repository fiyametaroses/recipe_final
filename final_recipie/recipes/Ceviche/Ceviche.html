Ceviche

Ingridients

  • 1 pound of sushi grade-fish
  • 1 cup of fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 tea spoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 2 ears of corn

How to Make It:

step 1

Use fresh, high-quality fish or seafood Cut the fish or seafood into small, bite-sized pieces.
Make sure they are uniform in size to ensure even "cooking.

step 2

Place the fish or seafood pieces in a non-reactive (glass or ceramic) bowl.
Squeeze enough lime and/or lemon juice to completely cover the fish. The acidity of the citrus juice "cooks" the fish by denaturing the proteins. Make sure the fish is completely submerged.
Season with salt. This not only adds flavor but also helps with the "cooking" process.
Let the fish marinate in the citrus juice for about 15-30 minutes. The exact time will depend on personal preference and the type of fish or seafood used. You'll notice the fish start to turn opaque as it "cooks" in the acidic juice.

step 3

While the fish is marinating, prepare the vegetables and seasonings.
Thinly slice the onion and soak it in cold water for a few minutes to reduce its sharpness. Drain before using.
You can also dice tomatoes, avocado, cucumber, or sweet peppers to add to the ceviche for extra flavor and texture..

step 4

Add the sliced onions, chopped chili peppers, cilantro,
and any other vegetables or seasonings you're using to the bowl with the fish.
Gently toss everything together until well combined.
Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more salt or lime juice if needed.
Serve the ceviche immediately, garnished with additional cilantro or avocado slices if desired

step 5

Ceviche is typically served chilled or at room temperature
It's often accompanied by tortilla chips, crackers, or toasted bread.
Enjoy your fresh and flavorful ceviche as a refreshing appetizer or light meal!
